### The Future of Enterprise Applications: Agentic Process Fabric and Modernizing Legacy Systems

The other thing that we're beginning to see happen, and what we've been thinking a lot about at Pega, is where we think the world is going.  I actually think that we're entering a world increasingly where our users and our customers aren't going to interact with applications in the way that they used to.

Right now, the way we make our users interact with applications, has anybody ever walked through a contact center? Anybody ever spent time inside a contact center? The hellish life of a contact center front end, right? Oftentimes, contact center users will have four, five, six different applications they need to go to because they have one application that does one thing and they have another application to do another thing. So often we actually put that experience not only on our users, we shove it out to our customers where you have to call one number if you want to get one service and got to go talk to another group. We're exposing our organization and system silos to our customers.

I actually think one of the big transformations that agents are going to drive is a lot of that complexity can now be hidden. Because instead of thinking of our business as a collection of applications that a user needs to switch between, I want to increasingly think about my business as a catalog, a fabric. That fabric consists of workflows, things that I can do on behalf of my employee or on behalf of my customer. That fabric consists of knowledge. What do I know that could answer a question for them? What kind of things are covered by my insurance policy? And that fabric consists of data. What are the actual pieces of information that a customer might want to see? What was my last bill on my policy? When is it due?

If I can take and make that fabric available and weave those workflows together, we do this through what we call our Agentic Process Fabric.
